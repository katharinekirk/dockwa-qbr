Expand Transient Self-Service via Improved Rate Management

Tyler Kneisel
Make 95% of the marketplace "rate ready" by end of Q2 through Rates v3 UX improvements, proper analytics instrumentation, and a marina nurturing program, while establishing clear marketplace ownership.

Measurable Outcomes

  • 95% of marketplace marinas have current, valid rates configured
  • Amplitude instrumentation live on rate configuration flows
  • Inquiry-to-booking conversion rate improved (baseline to be established in April)
  • Implementation team hours spent on OTA onboarding reduced toward zero

Why This Matters

The network strategy depends on converting claimed/published marinas into transacting marinas. Today, 2,780 marinas are published and can receive inquiries, but only a fraction can accept transient bookings because rates aren't configured. Rate setup is the critical gate between "published" and "bookable."

Milestones

A. Rates v3: UX Improvements (Product/Eng)
  • Address known UX friction in rate configuration that causes marina drop-off
  • Amplitude event instrumentation on Rates v2 (currently zero events tracked per Mike's audit)
  • Chameleon-guided tour for first-time rate setup (create season, set strategy, map to calendar)
  • Target: measurable improvement in rate setup completion rate
B. Rates v3: Proper Data Logging (Tyler + Eng)
  • Amplitude events implemented across rate configuration, modification, and deletion
  • Baseline established for rate setup funnel (start > complete > go live)
  • Data available for product team to identify and address drop-off points
C. Marina Nurturing Program (Tyler + Marketing)
  • Systematic outreach to published marinas without rates configured for 2026
  • Defined nurturing workflow: identify > outreach > guide through setup > confirm bookable
  • Coordination with MSM team on proactive rate-readiness touchpoints during pre-season calls
  • Progress tracked against 95% rate-ready target
D. Marketplace Ownership: Documented & Operationalized
  • Documented owner role and responsibilities for marketplace health
  • Marketplace ground rules operationalized
  • Regular reporting cadence on marketplace health metrics: rate readiness, inquiry response rate, booking conversion
  • Escalation path for rate discrepancy and policy violations

Key Dependencies & Risks

  • Engineering Capacity Constraints: Product/Eng capacity for Rates v3 Amplitude instrumentation and UX work is required. Chameleon integration for guided tour implementation needed.
  • Self-Serve Flow Staging: The self-serve "claimed to bookable" flow involves technical work across feature flags, Salesforce sync, and processing fee configuration. If engineering capacity is consumed by Service MVP, the full self-serve path may need to be staged.
  • Rate-Ready Target Aggressiveness: The 95% rate-ready target is aggressive. Establishing the baseline in April will tell us whether this is realistic or aspirational. Recommend treating it as directional with a "best achievable" framing.

Enterprise Team and Implementation Playbook Established

Matt Fradette
Define and operationalize the enterprise customer lifecycle from contract through implementation through ongoing success, including the team structure, playbook, feature requirements, and hiring plan to support it.

Measurable Outcomes

  • Enterprise implementation intake process standardized and deployed for all new enterprise accounts
  • Enterprise playbook documented and in use for active deals
  • Enterprise feature gap clearly mapped with delivery timelines
  • Accounting integration implementation time measured and baselined

Why This Matters

Dockwa is signing larger, more complex marina groups that require structured implementation, accounting integrations, POS configuration, and multi-location management. Our current enterprise deals already expose gaps in how we sell, onboard, and support these accounts. The enterprise pod also serves as a proving ground for building best practices and operational standards across implementation, support, and product delivery that we can fold back into the core mid-market organization over time.

A. Enterprise Contracts: Must-Haves and Principles (Matt + Legal)

B. Enterprise Implementation POV (Matt + Jared)

  • Defined SLAs: timeline expectations, resource commitments, escalation paths

Discussion Notes

Engineering Capacity is the Binding Constraint

Engineering capacity is the binding constraint across Rocks 3, 4, and 5. Service MVP (Rock 3) is the highest-revenue-impact bet. Self-serve transient infrastructure (Rock 4) and enterprise features (Rock 5) both need engineering time. Need explicit sequencing and trade-off decisions.

Rock 5 Scope Consideration

Rock 5 is the broadest rock. It covers contracts, implementation process, feature requirements, hiring, and accounting integrations. Consider whether this should be two rocks (Enterprise Sales Readiness + Enterprise Implementation Process) to improve accountability and tracking.

Rate-Ready Target Baseline

The 95% rate-ready target (Rock 4) is aggressive. Establishing the baseline in April will tell us whether this is realistic or aspirational. Recommend treating it as directional with a "best achievable" framing.
