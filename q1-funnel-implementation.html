Observations

Backlog is improving but still above target

  • 30-day backlog ratio hit 0.76:1 (on track). 90-day ratio is 1.11:1 (off track but trending down).
  • The improvement is partly driven by lower February case volume (43% below forecast), not just increased throughput.
  • If case volume returns to January levels (75% above forecast), the current team can only close ~65% of cases opened per month.

Success rate at 86.2% with a mix of decline reasons

  • 169 completed ($454K ARR) vs. 27 declined ($50K ARR) in Q1 so far.
  • Decline causes are a blend: Product Limitations (QuickBooks Desktop, POS for service/parts), KB2S (ownership changes, wrong product fit), and Failure to Launch (no staff buy-in).
  • Shake-a-Leg Miami (all 5 modules declined) was a clear bad-fit sale. Worth flagging to sales as a customer type to screen for.

26.6% of backlog marinas have only 1 module left

  • 10 marinas have only Integrations remaining; 7 marinas have only Transient remaining. These are flagged for focus to close ASAP.
  • This is a good leading indicator that throughput focus is working on the tail end of implementations.

Fuel module is a major outlier on implementation time

  • Q1 average: 170.3 days vs. TTM average of 89.6 days. This is nearly double the TTM and 2.4x the overall Q1 average of 70.4 days.
  • Worth understanding if this is a handful of stuck cases or a systemic issue with Fuel implementations.

Post-implementation support volume is high (403 cases T90)

  • V1-to-V2 QBO migration cases represent 33% of Q1 non-IS volume. Additional maps requests are 23%.
  • These are essentially implementation rework, not new implementation. They compete for the same team's attention.

Rock progress is mixed

  • 2 of 6 sub-items complete (standardize metrics, map workflow). 1 on track. 1 off track. 2 TBD/in progress.
  • The off-track item (quantify impact of top bottlenecks) was delayed by month-end escalations on accounting, reporting, and integrations.
  • This is the pattern: operational fires pull time away from the strategic Rock work.

Questions & Responses

How are we tracking to increasing baseline per-rep capacity?

The Pulse data shows 203 closed cases in 90 days (roughly 63/month).

We're currently making minor improvements to increase capacity, but there are blockers related to staffing that may impact our progress (Jake offboarding 4/2 and needing a backfill hired, Emma with 2 weeks of PTO in March, Richie with 1 week of PTO in March, Torey just starting ramp-up). To make major improvements with baseline per-rep capacity, major processes and tooling infrastructure will need to be changed to optimize how we create, assign, and manage cases to close, which could take weeks to months to implement and adapt to the new implementation experience internally. This is outlined in my bottleneck analysis doc.

What is driving the Fuel module implementation time of 170 days?

This is 2.4x the Q1 overall average. Is it a few stuck cases or a systemic issue? What would fix it?

This is a result of 3 Fuel modules finally ending implementation in January (out of 7 total fuel modules closed in Q1) that took between 208 and 352 days to complete. Common threads between the outliers: - all of them are year-round marinas sold on multi-module deals (5 modules each) to consolidate systems… usually transient / contracts / storage is prioritized for implementation before moving onto POS and/or Fuel. - each happened to be sold by Becca

What happened with the Rock item on quantifying top bottlenecks?

Is this recoverable before EOQ?

This is now complete. This was delayed due to managing integration implementation & support requests.

Are there any declined implementations that should have been screened out earlier in the sales process?

Shake-a-Leg Miami (sailing fleet, no staff buy-in) seems like a bad-fit sale. Is there a pattern we should flag to Matt's team?

Shake-a-Leg Miami did seem like a bad fit for our usage of the product to start. Although the bad product-fit has been a rare occurrence as of recent, IS is noticing a different pattern that Sales should be aware of: when there isn't a Dockwa "promoter" onsite at marina identified before CW, chances of Failure to Launch increase, as well as implementation time. Onsite operators not being on the same page as the decision makers in the sale led to the declines at Kent Narrows Boatel & Bayside Marine; Shake-a-Leg did churn partly because there was no onsite staff aware that Shake-a-Leg had purchased Dockwa, so there was no motive for them to work with us.
