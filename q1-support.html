Capacity & Ticket Management

Current baseline capacity sits at ~75 cases/day. Despite Katie's reduced IC quota as she steps into leadership, high-season targets hold capacity relatively flat at 75–78 cases/day, with Ais and Eric pushing to 19/day and Shakera filling volume as she ramps.

At peak (5-day work weeks), human solve capacity is targeted at 375–390 cases/week — which aligns almost exactly with the peak season forecast of ~390 cases/week (June–August, assuming 10% YoY ticket growth). This leaves no buffer, making FIN deflection a critical dependency for absorbing demand.

FIN Performance & Mobile Initiative

FIN is performing well on desktop, with an 80%+ resolution rate when engaging on boater support cases. However, engagement is limited to ~4 conversations/day because boaters conduct the vast majority of their business — reservations, payments, trip planning — in our mobile app (roughly 68% of all boater reservations originate from iOS or Android). Since we don't offer in-app support today, boaters are forced to exit the app entirely and call or email, bypassing FIN altogether.

A Q2 initiative has been proposed to integrate Intercom's mobile SDK into our iOS and Android apps, putting FIN directly in the path of our highest-volume support audience. Given the peak season capacity math above, mobile FIN deflection is not a nice-to-have — it is the primary mechanism by which the team can ensure it manages peak demand without adding headcount.
