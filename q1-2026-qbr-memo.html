SynthesisCross-Cutting Themes for QBR Discussion

1. ARR is strong, but growth quality mix deserves scrutiny

  • Expansion is overperforming (226% ARR attainment) and masking a new business slowdown (77.4% ARR attainment).
  • Expansion ACV is $2,665 vs $4,917 budget, suggesting lots of small module adds rather than strategic upsells.
  • If expansion normalizes, the new business pipeline gap becomes the binding constraint on growth.

2. Activation is the #1 retention lever and it's stuck

  • Modules that don't activate drive 58% of churn. Activation stall rates are 70-93% across products.
  • Implementation is overloaded (75% of team above forecast caseload).
  • The retention ceremonies rock is "on track" but hasn't moved the needle yet.
  • Key QBR question: what's the time-to-impact on activation initiatives, and is it fast enough?

3. Downgrades are an unbudgeted bleed

  • $69K in downgrades across 15 accounts with zero budgeted.
  • Needs root cause analysis: module-specific? Account-type-specific? Broader dissatisfaction signal?

4. Marketing funnel is underdelivering on expansion

  • Expansion MQLs at 19% of plan (12 vs 63 target). Expansion playbook is still missing per Pulse meeting.
  • Sales is self-sourcing NB effectively (SQL at 128% of NB budget), but expansion marketing is nearly absent.
  • This needs to become a Q2 priority if it isn't already.

5. Boater business is falling short

  • Dockwa+ has missed budget every month this quarter. 370 subscribers short of target.
  • Fuel deals are essentially not launched. Subscriber acquisition or retention (or both) is lagging.
  • Needs clarity on what's been tried and what's blocking faster growth.
