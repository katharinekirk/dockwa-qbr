Questions & Responses

What is driving the NB slowdown in Feb/Mar? Pipeline quality issue or conversion issue?

  • Feb softness was real but context-dependent, not a structural break. February closed at 14 of 16 logos — a dip that was largely a hangover from an outsized January, which benefited from accounts warmed and worked in Q4.
  • March is tracking ahead of plan, which reframes the narrative. At the halfway point of the month, we're at 74% to budget on a logo basis and forecasting to exceed 100% for March — indicating the NB funnel has been rebuilt and the Feb dip was timing-related, not indicative of a demand problem.
  • The underlying issue to watch is quality connects, not volume. 62% of Q1 prospecting activity yielded poor engagement (ie- no connect with decision makers or people of influence at the marina), and ghosting/disengagement after discovery or demo was the leading closed-lost theme. These are qualification issues at entry that need to be addressed even as the topline recovers.
  • Great Lakes remain a specific risk. 28 opps created, 1 closed won, 8 closed lost, 19 stalled — this market has structural conversion challenges and the open pipeline here is flattering funnel optics

Can you break down the 188 expansion CW logos? How many are proactive upsells vs renewals that added a module?

  • Safe Harbor deal is included in some scorecards The February spike (150 expansion logos in a single month) and the depressed expansion ACV ($2,665 vs. $4,917 budget — 54%) are explained by the Safe Harbor Marinas enterprise license deal. That contract was logged as approximately 100 individual location-level records, each appearing as a separate CW expansion logo in the CRM. These are sub-records of a single enterprise agreement, not 100 independent upsells.
  • Actual proactive upsells is 60-66% of plan (QTD) Proactive upsells exclude renewals from strict price increases who are not adding modules. We budgeted that 100 modules would be sold to the install base across 45 unique marinas in Q1. Sales has sold 60 add on modules- 60% of plan (excluding SHM) across 30 unique marinas (66% of plan). We are able to sell, on average, 2 additional modules to a marina, but we're not upselling as many marinas as we anticipated and at lower ACV.

What is the March close forecast?

The March committee forecast is currently projected at approximately 100% of plan, totaling $203K in Gross ARR.

  • New Business: Forecasted at 113% of budget, indicating overperformance against plan.
  • Expansion: Forecasted at 84% of budget, reflecting a shortfall relative to target.

Overall performance is being supported by strength in New Business, offsetting underperformance in Expansion. There are still several expansion deals that have potential to pull into the month that would increase Expansion performance materially.
