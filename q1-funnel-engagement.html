Marketing Q&A

What would it take to stand up a systematic expansion MQL engine for Q2?

The building blocks exist. The work is activating them in the right sequence.

Segmentation first

The HubSpot data model already has what we need: current module subscriptions, ARR, feature adoption opportunity, and engagement history. The first step is building dynamic segments that identify which customers are expansion-ready, filtered by module gap + feasibility, tenure, product engagement. Everything downstream depends on this.

Lead scoring

A scoring framework layered on customer engagement signals (email, site behavior, in-app activity) and buyer intent data in HubSpot will surface high-potential accounts for Sales. The goal is a prioritized view of the install base rather than asking them to work through it without signal.

Evergreen programs

Drop the Lines was one segment, one season. The next phase is lifecycle programs that run automatically: enrollment triggers based on customer profile and behavior, email sequences built around specific upsell paths and seasonality, and in-app messaging via Intercom/Chameleon to reach customers inside the platform.

Sales coordination

Expansion MQLs need a different handoff than new business. The infrastructure needs to give Sales full context on each account: what modules they have, what they're missing, and what triggered the flag. The goal is targeted outreach vs. generic.

Dockwa+ deal supply

A portion of this quarter's focus goes toward supporting the Dockwa+ motion, specifically driving deal supply from existing marina customers and supporting boater activation and retention efforts. Rather than treating this as a separate workstream, the plan is to build it into the lifecycle programs and product marketing cadences we're already constructing. It's a natural fit and shouldn't require significant additional lift once the infrastructure is in place.

The honest constraint: the HubSpot/SFDC sync has gaps and expansion tracking isn't fully reliable yet. Marketing ops work is running in parallel to address this. It won't block progress, but it will affect how quickly we can close the attribution loop.

Sales Q&A

What is behind the expansion opp shortfall? Is this a targeting problem, a handoff from CS issue, or a coverage gap?

Expansion Pipeline Creation Driven by Top-of-Funnel Gap:

Conversion from Expansion Accounts Worked to Opportunities remains on plan; however, only ~40% of install base accounts are being engaged vs. target, indicating a primary top-of-funnel activity shortfall, potentially compounded by CRM hygiene issues. Immediate focus is required to increase ToFu coverage—initial steps underway in March with a dedicated SDR targeting priority accounts during key selling windows.

MSMs Leading Expansion Opportunity Sourcing in Q1:

MSMs generated 54% (28/52) of Expansion opportunities in Q1, signaling a strong and scalable motion. At the same time, this success also highlights the gap in AE/SDR engagement, reinforcing the need to increase broader customer coverage to drive pipeline growth.
